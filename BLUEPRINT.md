## 🌌 Core Premise (Revised)

Everything happens in **Kali Yuga — the present dark age**.

The world is in collapse. Dharma stands on one leg. The signs have been appearing for centuries.
And now — **Kalki is born** in the village of Shambhala.

The other Yugas are gone. But they are not forgotten. The **Seven Chiranjeevis** — immortal beings
who have lived through all four ages — are still walking the earth, carrying the memories,
wounds, and wisdom of every era. One by one, drawn by divine will, they find Kalki.

They come to **protect him, test him, train him, and prepare him** — each bringing a fragment of
the past as a lesson. Through their memories, the player glimpses the other Yugas as
**Vision Sequences** — not travel, but inherited truth.

The story ends at the **Final Battle with Kali** (the demon king of the age, not the goddess).
Everything before it is preparation. Everything in it is consequence.

> **"You do not go looking for the Chiranjeevis. They have been waiting for you since before you were born."**

## 👹 The Villain Compendium — Kali & His Forces

> *Kali is not introduced at the end. He is woven into every chapter — first as symptoms,
> then as shadow, then as voice, then as face.*

### 🔴 KALI — The Demon Sovereign of the Age

**True Nature:** In Hindu mythology, Kali (कलि) is not a single demon but the personification
of the Kali Yuga itself — the spirit of discord, ego, and impurity. He is not brute force.
He is **slow corruption**. He wins by making people stop believing Dharma is possible.

**Physical Form:** Appears differently to each person — to a king, as a minister who whispers
greed. To a warrior, as the voice that says "strike first." His true form (revealed only in
Act IV) is vast, dark, crowned in stolen temple gold, eyes like eclipsed suns.

**Voice:** Never angry. Always patient. He has been winning for thousands of years.
He speaks in half-truths and comfortable lies.

**Motivation:** He does not want to destroy the world. He wants to **rule it at its lowest**.
A world of comfortable compromise, forgotten values, and small lives. He finds the idea of
a Golden Age exhausting and wasteful.

**Reveal Arc:**
```
Prologue  → Never named. His effects are everywhere.
Act I     → His agents appear. Violence without reason. Order without justice.
Act II    → Chiranjeevis name him. Vibhishana maps his network.
Act III   → Kali speaks for first time (through the betrayer). Sends a vision directly.
Act IV    → Physical confrontation. Full voice. The offer. The truth. The end.
```

### ⚫ THE SIX LIEUTENANTS — Kali's Named Forces

Each lieutenant represents one of the six vices (Arishadvarga) that Kali weaponizes.
They appear as **human-adjacent beings** — not monsters, but powerful corrupted figures.
Each one is encountered (and can be fought, reasoned with, or exposed) before the final battle.

| # | Lieutenant | Vice Embodied | First Appears | Role | Fate Options |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Adharmendra** | *Ego / False Authority* | Act I Scene 1.3 | Supreme General — a once-dharmic king turned warlord | Defeated in Act IV / Redeemed by Vibhishana's counsel |
| 2 | **Lobha** | *Greed* | Act II Scene 2.11 | Controls merchant guilds, buys kings, corrupts trade routes | Exposed by Vibhishana / Fought in Act III Trial 1 |
| 3 | **Krodha** | *Wrath* | Act III Scene 3.8 | Turns warriors against each other — fuels civil wars | Confronted in Act III / Redirected by Parashurama's lesson |
| 4 | **Moha** | *Delusion / False Dharma* | Act II Scene 2.8 | Most dangerous — creates fake religious movements to serve Kali | Exposed through Vyasa's wisdom / Cannot be fought directly |
| 5 | **Mada** | *Arrogance / Intoxication* | Act III Scene 3.9 | The betrayer's handler — seduces with power and glamour | Revealed during the Betrayal scene |
| 6 | **Matsarya** | *Envy* | Act IV Scene 4.3 | Kali's illusionist — makes Kalki doubt his own companions | Identified and broken by Memory Vision clarity |

---

### 🔴 KOKA & VIKOKA — Twin Warlords (Sub-Bosses)

From ancient tradition — Koka and Vikoka are twin demon generals, ancient beyond the Kali Yuga,
who chose Kali's service at the turn of the ages. They are Kali's **enforcers**:
- **Koka** — commands Kali's outer armies. Brutal, direct, honorable in a warped way.
  He respects Kalki's sword arm. He can be challenged to single combat.
- **Vikoka** — commands Kali's interior agents. Never appears in person until Act IV.
  His face is never seen. Possibly the same figure who delivers Kali's vision in Act III.

They are confronted in **Act IV Scene 4.2** (Koka, the outer wall) and **Act IV Scene 4.5**
(Vikoka, the inner sanctum split).

## 📊 Player State Variables

```
KARMA           (0–100)  : Moral compass. High = Dharmic path. Low = Adharmic choices.
DHARMA_POWER    (0–100)  : Kalki's readiness for battle. Grows as Chiranjeevis train him.
ADHARMA_FOG     (0–100)  : World corruption spreading. Rises if Kalki delays or errs.
COMPANIONS      []       : Chiranjeevis currently with Kalki. Affects scene options.
MEMORIES        {}       : Key choices across the journey — referenced in final act.
KALKI_NATURE    (warrior | sage | wanderer) : Set in Prologue. Colors prose & choices.
ENDINGS_FLAGS   {}       : Tracks threshold crossings for ending resolution.
```

## PROLOGUE: THE STATE OF THE WORLD (3 Scenes)
> *Before Kalki. The world as it is. The world as it has fallen.*

**Scene P1 — "The World Without Dharma"**
- The player is shown the state of Kali Yuga through the eyes of **ordinary people**:
  a farmer whose land is barren, a teacher whose students no longer listen,
  a warrior who fights only for gold.
- No choices yet — pure immersive prose that sets the stakes.
- ⚡ This is the "why" of everything that follows.

**Scene P2 — "The Signs"**
- Ancient texts are being read in a crumbling ashram. A child is about to be born.
- Choices: What does the reader of the signs believe?
  - `[He will come with fire]` → Sets world tone to urgent/martial
  - `[He will come with wisdom]` → Sets world tone to contemplative/prophetic
  - `[He will come quietly, and no one will notice until it is done]` → Sets world tone to subtle/grounded
- ⚡ This colours the prose voice of the entire game.

**Scene P3 — "Shambhala"**
- The village of Shambhala. A child is born. A white horse is seen at the edge of the forest.
- Player names/claims Kalki's nature:
  - `[The Warrior]` → KARMA 60, force-biased choices
  - `[The Sage]` → KARMA 50, wisdom-biased choices
  - `[The Wanderer]` → KARMA 40, observation-biased choices

## ACT I: THE BIRTH & THE SILENCE (8 Scenes)
> *Kalki grows. The world worsens. No Chiranjeevi has come yet. He is alone.*
> *👹 Villain Thread: Adharmendra's forces are present but unnamed — pure menace, no explanation.*

**Goal**: Establish Kalki's character, relationships, and initial understanding of his
destiny — before any divine guidance arrives. Also: seed the villain through *effects*,
not exposition. The player should feel the enemy before they know its name.

| Scene | Title | What Happens | Villain Thread | Key Choices |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1.1 | "The Father's House" | Kalki as child; Vishnuyasha tries to explain destiny | A travelling merchant speaks of a warlord burning ashrams to the east | Trust your father / Question everything / Ignore it |
| 1.2 | "The Horse at Dawn" | Devadatta appears at the edge of the forest | Devadatta came from the south — where the smoke is rising | Approach alone / Wake the village / Observe from the door |
| 1.3 | "The First Violence" | **Adharmendra's soldiers** raid Shambhala's edge — no banner, no reason given | First look at Adharmendra's forces — armoured, efficient, soulless | Fight unskilled and lose / Hide the villagers / Reason with the soldiers |
| 1.4 | "After the Defeat" | Kalki is injured. First real doubt. | One soldier stayed behind — he looked ashamed. Was he? | Rage at the sky / Accept the loss / Vow in silence |
| 1.5 | "The Mother's Wisdom" | Sumati tends wounds; tells the story of a king who sold his conscience | Sumati knows Adharmendra's name — but will not speak it | Listen fully / Ask only what you need / Tell her you already know |
| 1.6 | "The Village Elder" | The elder describes a man who passed through Shambhala 30 years ago with an axe | The elder also remembers when this warlord's men first appeared — "they came quietly at first" | Ask about the man / Ask about the old days / Ask what is coming |
| 1.7 | "The Night Devadatta Speaks" | Devadatta shows Kalki visions — war, darkness, a crowned shadow on a throne | The crowned shadow — Kali's silhouette, never revealed clearly | Follow vision south / Follow north / Stay and wait |
| 1.8 | "The Silence Before" | Final quiet moment. Shambhala feels like it is holding its breath. | Distant drums — Adharmendra's army is moving. Parashurama arrives at dawn. | Pray / Train alone / Walk to the edge of the village |

**No Chiranjeevi is present in Act I.** This is intentional — it makes their arrival feel earned.
**Adharmendra is felt but never named in Act I.** He is named by Vibhishana in Act II.

## ACT II: THE ARRIVAL OF GURUS (14 Scenes)
> *They come one by one. Each carries a different piece of the world's truth.*

**Goal**: Parashurama, Hanuman, Vyasa, and Vibhishana arrive and shape Kalki —
through training, through stories, through **Memory Visions** of the past Yugas.

---

### 🔴 PARASHURAMA ARRIVES (Scenes 2.1–2.4)

**Scene 2.1 — "The Man with the Axe"**
- An old man arrives at Shambhala. He carries no softness.
- He watches Kalki for three days without speaking.
- Choice: `[Approach first]` / `[Wait for him]` / `[Ask the elder who he is]`
- ⚡ How Kalki approaches determines Parashurama's teaching style throughout.

**Scene 2.2 — "The First Lesson"**
- Parashurama begins training. Brutal. No praise. Only correction.
- Choice: `[Endure without complaint]` / `[Push back]` / `[Ask why he trains this way]`

**Scene 2.3 — 🔮 MEMORY VISION: "The 21 Dynasties" (Satya Yuga)**
- Parashurama shares his deepest memory: the moment he chose righteous destruction.
- The player experiences it as Parashurama's eyes — watching the choice unfold.
- Choice: `[Judge him]` / `[Understand him]` / `[Ask if he regrets it]`
- ⚡ This sets MEMORIES['parashu_judgment'] — echoes throughout the game.

**Scene 2.4 — "What the Axe Remembers"**
- Parashurama gives Kalki his first real weapon. A test of worthiness follows.
- Choice: `[Take it with reverence]` / `[Refuse — you have Devadatta]` / `[Ask what it cost him]`

---

### 🟡 HANUMAN ARRIVES (Scenes 2.5–2.7)

**Scene 2.5 — "The Wind Before the Rain"**
- A strange wind bends all trees toward the south for a day.
- At dusk, Hanuman stands at the gate of Shambhala.
- Choice: `[Bow immediately]` / `[Ask why he has come]` / `[Look to Parashurama for a sign]`

**Scene 2.6 — 🔮 MEMORY VISION: "The Burning of Lanka" (Treta Yuga)**
- Hanuman shows Kalki the moment his tail was set ablaze — and what he did next.
- Not fear. Not anger. Something else entirely.
- Choice: `[What gave you the courage?]` / `[What held you back from destroying it all?]` / `[Were you afraid?]`
- ⚡ Sets MEMORIES['hanuman_lesson'] — devotion vs. power.

**Scene 2.7 — "Companion"**
- Hanuman declares he will travel with Kalki. Not as a servant. As a brother.
- Choice: `[Accept with humility]` / `[Ask what he expects in return]` / `[Accept with joy]`

---

### ⚪ VYASA ARRIVES (Scenes 2.8–2.10)

**Scene 2.8 — "The Old Man Who Knows Everything"**
- Vyasa arrives unannounced. He has already eaten breakfast and is waiting by the fire.
- He speaks to Kalki as if mid-conversation.
- Choice: `[Play along]` / `[Ask where he came from]` / `[Sit in silence and listen]`

**Scene 2.9 — 🔮 MEMORY VISION: "The Night of the Mahabharata" (Dvapara Yuga)**
- Vyasa shows the moment he chose to inscribe the full weight of human folly into permanent verse.
- Choice: `[Why did you record the failure too?]` / `[Was it worth it?]` / `[Who were you writing it for?]`
- ⚡ Sets MEMORIES['vyasa_truth'] — knowledge, grief, and purpose.

**Scene 2.10 — "The Map of What Is Coming"**
- Vyasa lays out the prophecy of Kali in full. No softening.
- The scale of what Kalki must face becomes clear.
- Choice: `[Accept the full weight]` / `[Ask if there is another way]` / `[Ask why it falls to you]`

---

### 🟢 VIBHISHANA ARRIVES (Scenes 2.11–2.13)
> 👹 *Villain Thread: Vibhishana names the enemy for the first time. Lobha and Moha introduced.*

**Scene 2.11 — "The Unlikely Advisor"**
- Vibhishana arrives bearing intelligence — he has been mapping Kali's network for decades.
- He **names Adharmendra** for the first time. He also names **Lobha** (the one who bought
  the northern kings) and **Moha** (the one who corrupted the temple networks).
- He draws a map of how Kali controls without fighting: bribery → religious compromise → silence.
- Choice: `[Believe all of it]` / `[Question how he knows]` / `[Ask why he waited this long]`
- ⚡ ADHARMA_FOG visibility increases — the player now sees how vast the problem is.

**Scene 2.12 — 🔮 MEMORY VISION: "The Exile from Lanka" (Treta Yuga)**
- Vibhishana shares the moment Ravana cast him out for speaking dharma.
- Ravana's court had the *same pattern* as Kali's — flattery, ego, and slow drowning of truth.
- Choice: `[Was it worth the exile?]` / `[Did you grieve Ravana?]` / `[How did you know you were right?]`
- ⚡ Sets MEMORIES['vibhishana_cost'] — and the player recognizes Lanka's fall as a *preview*
  of what Kali Yuga looks like at scale.

**Scene 2.13 — "Strategy Before Swords"**
- Vibhishana lays out a multi-front plan: expose Lobha's merchant network, pull Moha's
  false priests into the open, then move on Adharmendra's fortress.
- Choice: `[Accept the plan]` / `[Modify based on what Parashurama taught]` / `[Reject and move on instinct]`
- ⚡ This sets the MEMORIES['battle_strategy'] variable used heavily in Act IV.

**Scene 2.14 — "The Company of Four"**
- Kalki now has Parashurama, Hanuman, Vyasa, and Vibhishana.
- A rare quiet moment — they sit together. The full shape of the enemy is now visible.
- Vyasa says: *"Kali has six hands. We have named three. The others you will meet yourself."*
- ⚡ Each companion speaks — their words seed their unique dialogue threads for the rest of the game.
- ⚡ **Krodha, Mada, and Matsarya are foreshadowed but not named yet.**

## ACT III: THE GREAT TRIALS (12 Scenes)
> *Kripacharya and Ashwatthama. The world tested. Kalki tested.*

**Goal**: Three major trials before the final battle. Kripacharya teaches strategy.
Ashwatthama arrives as an unknown force. Kalki faces his own darkness.

---

### 🔵 KRIPACHARYA ARRIVES (Scenes 3.1–3.3)

**Scene 3.1 — "The General Who Survived"**
- Kripacharya arrives — the only Kuru-side veteran who survived Kurukshetra with his
  ethics intact. He does not speak of victory. He speaks of how to avoid massacre.
- Choice: `[Ask him to lead the army]` / `[Ask him to observe and advise]` / `[Ask what he failed at]`

**Scene 3.2 — 🔮 MEMORY VISION: "Kurukshetra: Day 18" (Dvapara Yuga)**
- The last day. Kripacharya's account of what war really costs.
- Choice: `[What would you have done differently?]` / `[Was Dhamma preserved?]` / `[Who was truly defeated?]`
- ⚡ Sets MEMORIES['kripacharya_war'] — the cost of necessary violence.

**Scene 3.3 — "The Formations"**
- Kripacharya teaches Vyuha (battle formations) in the context of Kalki's coming fight.
- Choice: Apply `[Chakravyuh — encirclement]` / `[Garuda — speed assault]` / `[Shakata — defensive hold]`
- ⚡ This choice affects the final battle scene options.

---

### ⚫ ASHWATTHAMA — THE WILD CARD (Scenes 3.4–3.7)

**Scene 3.4 — "The Wounded Thing"**
- Ashwatthama is found near Shambhala — bleeding from his cursed gem, confused.
- He is dangerous. His motives are unclear.
- Choice: `[Approach and offer water]` / `[Send Parashurama]` / `[Let Vyasa handle it]`

**Scene 3.5 — "Neither Friend Nor Enemy"**
- Ashwatthama does not trust Kalki. He does not trust anyone.
- He reveals Kali has been using him — feeding his pain, promising relief.
- Choice: `[Believe him]` / `[Test him]` / `[Let him speak without judgment]`

**Scene 3.6 — 🔮 MEMORY VISION: "The Last Night of Kurukshetra" (Dvapara Yuga)**
- Ashwatthama's perspective on the night he used the Brahmastra on the sleeping camp.
- The player experiences his grief, rage, and the curse that followed.
- Choice: `[Condemn the act]` / `[Understand it without condoning]` / `[Ask about the curse's pain first]`
- ⚡ Sets MEMORIES['ashwatthama_curse'] — the most consequential memory in the game.

**Scene 3.7 — "The Choice for Ashwatthama"**
- Kalki must decide what happens to Ashwatthama.
  - `[Trust him and bring him into the company]` → He joins; Wild Card resolved positively; rare endings unlocked
  - `[Acknowledge him but keep distance]` → He follows at the edge; shows up unexpectedly in Act IV
  - `[Send him away]` → He vanishes; returns as a neutral force in the final battle

### 🌑 THREE TRIALS (Scenes 3.8–3.12)
> 👹 *Villain Thread: Named lieutenants confront Kalki directly. Kali speaks for the first time.*

**Scene 3.8 — "The First Trial: The City of Compromise"**
- A major city runs under **Lobha's** control — the king was not conquered, he was bought.
- Lobha himself is here: polished, reasonable, generous. He offers Kalki a deal.
  *"Why make war? Take Shambhala's peace and I will leave it untouched."*
- **Krodha** is Lobha's enforcer — he is in the courtyard below, keeping the people afraid.
- Choice: `[Challenge Lobha publicly in the king's court]` / `[Find the one advisor who still holds dharma]` / `[Let Vibhishana negotiate — he has done this before]`
- ⚡ ADHARMA_FOG changes based on outcome. If Lobha is exposed, Krodha attacks openly.

**Scene 3.9 — "The Second Trial: The Betrayal"**
- **Mada** — Kali's lieutenant of arrogance and seduction — has been working on one of
  Kalki's companions. The compromised companion leaks the army's location.
- Who is it? → Determined by the **lowest relationship score** in COMPANIONS array.
- The companion is not evil — they were shown a version of the future where Kali's way
  seemed less costly than Kalki's. Mada gave them certainty; Kalki gave them doubt.
- Choice: `[Confront them alone]` / `[Expose them to the full group]` / `[Give them a chance to confess first]`
- ⚡ Most emotionally complex scene. Sets MEMORIES['the_betrayer'] — key for endings.
- ⚡ Mada disappears after this scene. He re-appears in Act IV as Matsarya (illusionist form).

**Scene 3.10 — "The Third Trial: Kali's Voice"**
- **Kali speaks to Kalki directly for the first time** — not in person, but through a vision
  that arrives uninvited. His voice is calm. He is not threatening. He is disappointed.
- The vision: a future where Kalki wins and then — slowly, inevitably — builds a new
  tyranny from the wreckage. *"You and I are made of the same age. You cannot burn me
  without burning yourself."*
- Choice: `[Reject the vision entirely]` / `[Ask Kali how you prevent becoming him]` / `[Sit with the discomfort and say nothing]`
- ⚡ Sets MEMORIES['kali_first_contact'] and MEMORIES['kalki_darkness'] — determines
  what dialogue Kali opens with in the final confrontation.

**Scene 3.11 — "The Eve of the March"**
- The army gathers. Bali has not yet arrived. The six companions sit under an open sky.
- Vikoka — Kali's shadow general — sends a messenger: *"He is ready for you."*
  The message is received without fear. The companions decide what it means.
- Choice: `[Give a speech — define what you are fighting for]` / `[Spend the night in prayer]` / `[Ask each companion what they fear most]`
- ⚡ The speech (if given) becomes the prose anchor for the True Ending epilogue.

**Scene 3.12 — "Bali Rises"**
- The ground shakes. From Patala — Bali ascends with his army of ancient kings.
- He has been waiting since Vamana's promise. He chose this day himself.
- 🔮 MEMORY VISION: *"Vamana's Three Steps"* — the day Bali gave everything and found
  that surrender, done with full dignity, is its own kind of sovereignty.
- Choice: `[Receive him as a sovereign equal]` / `[Bow before the weight of his sacrifice]` / `[Ask if he is truly certain — this is Kali's age, not his]`
- ⚡ Bali's full army only joins if KARMA > 60.
- ⚡ Bali knows Koka personally — they were enemies of old. He will fight Koka in Act IV.

## ACT IV: THE MARCH & THE FINAL BATTLE (10 Scenes)
> *All roads end here. Kali's stronghold. Every villain met. Every thread resolved.*
> 👹 *Villain Thread: Koka (outer fortress), Vikoka (inner sanctum), Moha (illusions),*
> *Adharmendra (the general), Kali himself — voice, face, offer, and final truth.*

**Goal**: March on Kali's stronghold. Every lieutenant is met. Every memory is tested.
Then face Kali himself — and end the age, or be consumed by it.

| Scene | Title | Villain Present | What Happens | Key Choices |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 4.1 | "The March Begins" | — | The army moves. People line the roads. Some throw flowers. Some spit. Some join. | Lead from front / Walk among them / Let Bali lead |
| 4.2 | "Kali's First Wall" | **Koka** | Outer fortress. Koka challenges Kalki to honourable single combat. Bali recognises him. | Fight Koka (Parashurama's path) / Send Bali / Use Kripacharya's Garuda formation |
| 4.3 | "The Field of Illusions" | **Matsarya / Moha** | The illusion field — false loved ones, false victories, false versions of Kali already defeated | Trust Vyasa's counter-sight / Trust Hanuman who cannot be deceived / Name the illusion aloud |
| 4.4 | "The General of the Age" | **Adharmendra** | Kali's Supreme General. A former dharmic king. He still remembers who he was. | Offer him the memory of himself / Fight without hesitation / Demand he stand aside |
| 4.5 | "The Inner Sanctum" | **Vikoka** | Companions split to hold the fortress — each faces Vikoka's shadow agents. Player decides who goes where. | Assign companions based on their strengths / Let them choose / Hold everyone together (costly) |
| 4.6 | "Kali's Offer" | **Kali (voice)** | In the throne room. Kali speaks before he is seen. Offers co-rulership of a "reasonable" age. | Refuse entirely / Listen without responding / Ask what he would actually preserve |

## 🔮 The Memory Vision System (Core Mechanic)

Memory Visions are **short branching flashback sequences** — not full acts, but 2–3 scene
glimpses into a past Yuga, experienced through a Chiranjeevi's consciousness.

They serve three purposes:
1. **Lore delivery** — the mythology of other Yugas without making the player "go there"
2. **Choice consequence** — player choices within the vision set MEMORIES that matter later
3. **Relationship deepening** — sharing a memory is an act of trust

**Vision Format:**
```
[Chiranjeevi Name] closes their eyes. A warmth spreads through the air between you.
And then — you are not here anymore.

You are in [Yuga / Place / Moment].

[2-3 paragraphs of immersive prose]

[1-2 choices that shape the memory and set a MEMORIES key]

The vision fades. You are in Shambhala again.
[Chiranjeevi] looks at you and says: "[Line that reflects your choice within the vision]"
```

- "Memory ends in..." subtle indicator — creates urgency

- Base: Tanpura drone (always present)

  - Dvapara → Veena, silence, the sound of armies waking
